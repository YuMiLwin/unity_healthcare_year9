Herpes


Herpes refers to a group of viral infections caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV). The most common types are oral herpes (HSV-1), which causes cold sores or fever blisters around the mouth, and genital herpes (HSV-2), which leads to sores in the genital and anal areas. Herpes is highly contagious and spreads through close contact, including sexual contact or kissing.

What are the symptoms of Herpes?


  • painful sores
  • itching
  • flu-like symptoms

How can we prevent?


    Safe sex practices, avoiding contact during outbreaks, and antiviral medications can reduce the risk of transmission.
