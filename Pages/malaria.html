Malaria


Malaria is a potentially life-threatening tropical disease caused by the Plasmodium parasite and transmitted through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It is characterized by symptoms such as high fever, chills, sweats, and flu-like illness. If not promptly treated, malaria can lead to severe complications, including organ failure and death.

What are the symptoms of Malaria?


  • fever
  • chills
  • fatigue

How can we prevent?


    Preventing malaria involves using insect repellent, bed nets, and taking antimalarial medications when traveling to endemic areas.
