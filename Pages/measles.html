Measles


Measles, also known as rubeola, is a highly contagious viral infection that primarily affects children but can occur in people of all ages. It causes symptoms like high fever, a characteristic red rash, cough, and runny nose. Measles can lead to serious complications, including pneumonia, encephalitis, and even death, especially in unvaccinated individuals.

What are the symptoms of Measles?


  • high fever
  • cough
  • runny nose

How can we prevent?


    Measles can be prevented through vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine.
